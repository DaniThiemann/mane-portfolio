[CLIENT BRIEF]
Next Play is a Brazilian esports broadcasting network — the first dedicated portal
for competitive gaming content in Brazil. Think SporTV or DAZN, but exclusively
for esports: League of Legends, Valorant, CS:GO championships, weekly highlights,
athlete interviews, commentator roundtables.

Brand role: Brand Designer
Project type: Full identity system + marketing campaign assets
Primary slogan: O PORTAL DOS E-SPORTS CHEGOU
Secondary tagline: O universo dos eSports na sua tela
Website: nextplay.com.br
Language: Brazilian Portuguese

Target audience: Brazilian gaming community, 16–30, highly online, esports-native.
Speak to them directly, as peers. Never corporate. Never passive.

[FULL BRAND SYSTEM]
→ Read the complete system from:
  assets/perproject/nextplay/NEXTPLAY_BRAND_SYSTEM.txt

Key visual rules to hold in working memory:

SYMBOL: Horizontally stretched hexagon, rounded corners, wider than tall.
This shape is the brand's portal device — it frames content, tiles as texture,
and contains the logo mark.

LOGO MARK: Bold geometric N + play-button chevron (►) = "NP►"
Contained version: NP► mark inside the hexagon shape.

COLORS:
  Brand Red    #E8322A  — primary, symbol, default logo
  Lime Green   #B2D422  — championships / competitive
  Deep Violet  #4B0FD5  — roundtable / analysis
  Near-Black   #0D0D0D  — backgrounds
  White        #FFFFFF  — on dark

TYPOGRAPHY:
  Impact headlines:  ABC Gravity XX Compressed (all-caps, bleed off edges)
  Brand display:     Monument Extended Bold
  Body/UI:           IBM Plex Sans (Light → Bold)
  Accent:            Poppins Bold

ICONS (always pair with their color):
  Flame    → Red    → Weekly highlights
  Swords   → Green  → Championships
  Headset  → Violet → Roundtable/commentary

TEXTURE: Hexagon honeycomb tile pattern (monochromatic, full-bleed backgrounds)

HARD RULES:
  - Never rotate the symbol or logo
  - Never mix colors within one symbol instance
  - Never use icons as logo replacements
  - Never low-contrast color combinations
  - Never alter the internal content of the contained mark
