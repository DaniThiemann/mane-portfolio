NEXT PLAY — BRAND DESIGN SYSTEM
================================
Brazilian Esports Broadcasting Network
Role: Brand Designer | Year: 2026
Category: Branding, Identity, Motion, Broadcast

================================================================================
1. BRAND OVERVIEW & POSITIONING
================================================================================

Next Play is Brazil's dedicated esports broadcasting portal — positioned as the
SporTV or DAZN of competitive gaming. It covers:
  - League of Legends, Valorant, CS:GO championships
  - Weekly highlight clips ("destaques da semana")
  - Athlete/player interviews
  - Commentator roundtable sessions ("mesa redonda")

Target audience: Brazilian gaming community, 16–30, highly online, culturally
fluent in esports. They know who "Aspas" is. They follow team rivalries.
They use esports vocabulary natively.

Primary slogan:  O PORTAL DOS E-SPORTS CHEGOU
Secondary tagline: O universo dos eSports na sua tela
CTA language: ASSISTA AO VIVO / CHEGOU / É AGORA

Competitive context: Not competing with traditional sports broadcasters.
Owning the esports vertical entirely in Brazil — first-mover identity.


================================================================================
2. VISUAL IDENTITY
================================================================================

--- SYMBOL ---
A horizontally stretched hexagon with heavily rounded corners — wider than tall,
like a lateral portal or broadcast lens. This is the brand's primary geometric
shape. It appears in three roles:
  (a) Standalone symbol: the bare hexagon shape in red
  (b) Contained logo: NP► mark locked inside the hexagon (for app icons, badges)
  (c) Graphic frame: hexagon outline overlaid on photography as a portal/window
      device — the shape frames the content within, reinforcing the "portal" concept

--- LOGO MARK ---
Two geometric, bold, rounded letterforms:
  N  — a wide geometric N with a diagonal stroke and rounded stroke terminals
  P► — the letter P replaced by a play-button chevron (►), sharing exact weight
       and corner radius with the N

Together they read "NP►" — the N for Next, the play button for broadcast.
The main logo is always the standalone NP► mark.
The contained version is NP► locked inside the hexagon.

Logo color variants (use whole files, never recolor):
  logomain_red.png    — primary brand application
  logomain_green.png  — championship / competitive contexts
  logomain_purple.png — roundtable / analysis contexts
  logomain_white.png  — on dark backgrounds

--- COLOR SYSTEM ---
Brand Red        #E8322A   Primary. Symbol, main logo, dominant poster color.
Electric Green   #B2D422   Championships / competitive. Lime, high-energy.
Deep Violet      #4B0FD5   Commentator / roundtable / analysis content.
Near-Black       #0D0D0D   Backgrounds, dark mode, dominant base.
White            #FFFFFF   Logo on dark backgrounds; text on dark.

Color pairing rules:
  Red on black       — main brand default
  Green on violet    — championship match promotion
  Violet on green    — inverted championship variant
  Red on black (photography) — editorial poster mode
  NEVER: low-contrast combinations (e.g. green on white, red on violet)
  NEVER: mix colors within a single symbol/logo instance

--- TYPOGRAPHY ---
Display / Full-bleed impact:
  ABC Gravity XX Compressed / Variable Condensed
  Use: massive all-caps headlines bleeding off edges, match promotion, raw energy

Brand display (on white/light grounds):
  Monument Extended Bold
  Use: "NEXT PLAY" at large scale, clean typographic lockups

Body / UI / Supporting:
  IBM Plex Sans — Light, Regular, Medium, Bold
  Use: all running text, labels, metadata, UI copy, captions

Accent:
  Poppins Bold
  Use: secondary headline supporting roles

--- CONTENT CATEGORY ICONS ---
Three icons form the content taxonomy system. Each icon is bold, rounded,
geometric — matching the weight of the logo mark. Each icon owns one color.

  FLAME icon     → Red    (#E8322A) → Weekly highlights / trending content
  CROSSED SWORDS → Green  (#B2D422) → Championships / competitive match coverage
  HEADSET icon   → Violet (#4B0FD5) → Roundtable / analyst desk sessions

Icon files: assets/perproject/nextplay/icons/[icon]_[color].png
Note: sword filename has a typo — it is "sowrd_[color].png" not "sword_"

--- PATTERN / TEXTURE ---
The stretched hexagon tiles into a dense honeycomb-mesh repeat pattern used as
full-bleed backgrounds. This is the brand's primary non-photographic texture.
It appears in all icon-showcase posters. When used monochromatically (e.g. violet
tiles on violet ground) it creates an embossed/tonal depth effect.

3. POSTER DESIGN LANGUAGE — THREE MODES
================================================================================

MODE 1: PHOTOGRAPHY (editorial/campaign)
  - Full-bleed dark photograph (esports player, crowd, fan group)
  - NP► contained logo mark centered at top (red)
  - Hexagon outline overlaid on photo as a graphic frame/portal device
  - Slogan or player name inside or near the hexagon
  - URL + slogan at bottom, white on dark
  Example: POSTER1.png (Aspas player from behind, hexagon outline over jersey)

MODE 2: TYPOGRAPHIC (match promotion / campaign)
  - Bold flat background (green or violet — never red for this mode)
  - Massive ABC Gravity Compressed headline text, bleeding off all edges
  - Hexagon shape as a cut-out window in the center, revealing a contrasting
    repeat-text pattern inside (brand name, "DIA DE JOGO", etc.)
  - "ASSISTA AO VIVO" + URL at bottom in same compressed type
  Example: PRONTO-26.png (violet), PRONTO-27.png (green)

MODE 3: ICON GRID (awareness / content taxonomy)
  - Full-bleed hexagon tile texture as background
  - Three content icons stacked vertically in tonal/embossed treatment
  - NP contained logo at top-right corner
  - Tagline at bottom: "O universo dos eSports na sua tela."
  - Color variants: red texture (PRONTO copy 4), violet texture (PRONTO copy 3),
    green texture (PRONTO copy 5)
  Example: PRONTO_Prancheta 1 cópia 3/4/5.png

MODE 4: FAN/COMMUNITY (warm, social-first)
  - Photography with purple/pink cinematic grade
  - Large "NEXT PLAY" wordmark (Monument Extended Bold) at top
  - Hexagon outlines used decoratively, not framing
  - Warmer, more inviting tone — entry-point for new audience
  Example: PRONTO-13.png (fan group watching a screen)


================================================================================
4. BRAND RULES (from usage direction slide — APNXTPLAY.png)
================================================================================

DO NOT:
  1. Divide or fragment the brand mark
  2. Place the symbol below the wordmark
  3. Rotate the brand mark or symbol
  4. Alter the internal content of the symbol (the NP► inside the hexagon)
  5. Apply any graphic mark as part of the symbol
  6. Use icons as a logo or directly alongside the symbol (without proper hierarchy)
  7. Alter icon positioning or scale relative to the brand mark
  8. Remove established icon elements
  9. Use low-contrast color combinations
  10. Use different colors on different elements within the same symbol instance

7. TONE OF VOICE
================================================================================

Language: Brazilian Portuguese (primary)
Energy: Direct, excited, unapologetic, community-peer
Not corporate. Not passive. Not hedged.

Imperative CTAs: ASSISTA AO VIVO, CHEGOU, VAI COMEÇAR, É AGORA
Descriptive: O portal dos e-sports, o universo dos e-sports, ao vivo, campeonato
Audience-aware: Uses esports vocabulary — atleta, clutch, pick, draft, mapa

Content pillar copy:
  Championships:     CAMPEONATO / DIA DE JOGO / ASSISTA AO VIVO
  Weekly highlights: DESTAQUES DA SEMANA / O QUE ROLOU / MELHORES MOMENTOS
  Roundtable:        MESA REDONDA / ANÁLISE / OS ESPECIALISTAS FALAM
